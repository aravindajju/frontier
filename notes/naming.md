# Naming

**Decided (July 2026): Frontier** — full title *Frontier: A Methodology for Forward Deployed Engineering*. Author's final call, overriding the collision-check score. The word carries the intended meaning: the edge where the product meets the unknown, where FDEs work.

Known risks accepted with this choice (from the round-2 check): "frontier models"/"frontier labs" is entrenched AI vocabulary (Frontier Model Forum: Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft), so "Frontier Engineering" can read as "engineering at frontier AI labs"; frontier.com is Frontier Communications, frontier.io is live, frontier.dev registered; Frontier Airlines, ORNL's Frontier supercomputer, and Frontier Developments crowd search. Mitigations: always use the full title with the subtitle; brand as "the Frontier methodology" / "Frontier FDE"; pick a distinctive domain (e.g., frontiermethod.*, frontier-fde.*, or a geeksonfeet subdomain) rather than chasing the bare word; trademark counsel before publishing.

Runners-up, documented for the record: Shipside (round 2 top score, 4/5), Fieldcraft (round 1 leader, 4/5).

## Round 2 (July 2026)

**Shipside — 4/5, leader.** The cleanest namespace of all twelve round-2 candidates. Collisions: an obscure maritime dictionary term, a 60-year-old Dutch tax-free-car-export firm on shipside.com, and one tiny web-dev agency on shipside.dev (no funding or press); shipside.io is registered but unused. The double meaning is exactly right for software people: *ship* (shipping code) + *-side* (at the customer's side, on location — like bedside, dockside). Grammar works everywhere: "a shipside engineer," "a Shipside engagement," "we run Shipside," "go shipside" (a pleasant echo of Lean's "go to the gemba"). Risks: a literal logistics reading on first contact; the small shipside.dev agency. Actions if chosen: register domains early, USPTO class 9/42 search, consider approaching the agency.

**Gemba Engineering — 3/5.** Best borrowed semantics (Toyota/Lean pedigree, "go to where the work happens"), the compound looks claimable — but the root word is saturated by Lean consulting and reads as manufacturing, and gemba.io/.dev are taken by others (one of them, gemba.gr, even describes the stay-close-to-the-work concept).

**Firsthand Engineering — 3/5.** The most self-explanatory candidate ("built from firsthand context"), compound unclaimed — but a $26M-funded AI startup named Firsthand (March 2025) and fully occupied domains crowd the root.

**Rejected in round 2:**
- **Away Team (2/5)** — Amazon already uses "away team model" as an engineering term meaning something different (a team modifying another team's codebase); disqualifying for a term of art.
- **Forefront (2/5)** — Microsoft Forefront's ghost still owns the word for enterprise-IT veterans; Forefront AI (forefront.ai) is active; all obvious domains taken; and the word carries none of the on-site meaning.
- **Frontier (1/5)** — "frontier models"/"frontier labs" hijacked the word in exactly our market; Frontier Communications owns frontier.com; unownable.
- **In Situ (2/5)** — Boeing's Insitu drone subsidiary sits on the trademark; cold and un-verbable.
- **Outpost (2/5)** — active fintech and Outpost24 collisions; an outpost holds position, it doesn't ship and feed back.
- **Beachhead (2/5)** — "beachhead market" is entrenched GTM vocabulary (Moore/Aulet); startup readers will hear market strategy, not engineering.
- **Sapper (1.5/5)** — Svelte's deprecated framework of the same name; reads as "SAP consultant" aloud.
- **Pathfinder (1/5)** — Paizo RPG, Nissan, Salesforce; namespace exhausted.
- **Expeditionary Engineering (1/5)** — exact name owned by an active San Diego defense-software firm (acquired by Ultra I&C, 2025).

## Round 1 (July 2026)

## Shortlist

**Fieldcraft — 4/5, best risk/reward.** No software methodology, book, or product uses it. The .com's former owner (an Austin agrifood startup) is defunct and its DNS is dead; .dev and .io are parked but not in use. The word is an established military term for the skills of operating in the field — which is the right metaphor, parallel to intelligence "tradecraft." Costs: survivalist/tactical brands (Fieldcraft Survival) dominate current SEO, and the word says nothing about software until the subtitle does. Ties cleanly to the geeksonfeet brand ("geeks on feet practicing engineering fieldcraft").

**Forward Deployed Engineering, as-is — 3/5.** The SRE-book play: the discipline name is the methodology name. Perfect discoverability (the term is surging), zero education needed. But the land grab is underway: a "Forward Deployed" Substack/podcast owns forwarddeployed.com, forwarddeployedengineering.com is a claimed placeholder, fde.academy sells certification, and at least five FDE books shipped or are in progress in 2025-26. We would be competing to become canonical rather than defining a fresh term, and the generic phrase can't be owned.

**Edgework — 3/5, sleeper.** Remarkably, edgework.com/.io/.dev all appear unregistered. It's a sociology term (Lyng: voluntary risk-taking) with an evocative double meaning against "overfit at the edge." Real cost: in tech, "edge" reads as edge computing, a genuine mismatch.

**Hybrid (recommended direction).** Coin the name for the methodology and keep the discipline term in the subtitle: *Fieldcraft: A Methodology for Forward Deployed Engineering*. The coined word gives ownability (domain, book title, "we run Fieldcraft"); the subtitle carries all the SEO and tells readers exactly what it is. This mirrors how "Shape Up" named Basecamp's method while "product development" stayed the discipline.

## Rejected

- **Deploy Forward (2/5)** — deployforward.dev is an active AI-adoption consultancy in the same conceptual space.
- **Field-Driven Development (2/5)** — FDD abbreviation belongs to Feature-Driven Development; nTop markets "Field-Driven Design."
- **Frontline Engineering (2/5)** — generic trade name used by many unrelated firms; trademark-crowded.
- **The Forward Method (1/5)** — collides with Euler's forward method, Hinton's Forward-Forward, and general genericity; domain already claimed.
- **Groundwork (2/5)** — GroundWork Monitor (IT monitoring product since 2004); Kant owns the book-title association; and the word means preparation *before* the work, which is the wrong meaning.
